Our mission

Space belongs to every curious kid.

Little Astronomers is a volunteer-led outreach program founded by student astronomer Vaanya Ahuja. We make real science feel playful, understandable, and possible—especially for young learners meeting astronomy for the first time.

01💭

Wonder first

Every lesson begins with a question worth chasing.

02👐

Learn by making

Kids build, test, notice, explain—and take their discoveries home.

03🌍

Open to everyone

Free, welcoming workshops built for schools, libraries, and camps.

Six stops across the universe

One complete cosmic curriculum.

Each workshop follows the same kid-friendly rhythm: wonder → mini-lesson → hands-on build → share → reflect.

01🪐
Solar System

Our Place in Space

Meet the planets, discover why humans explore, and make a wearable Solar System bracelet.

MAKE ITBead-and-string planet bracelet
02✨
Constellations

Stories in the Stars

Find patterns in the night sky and learn how cultures used stars for stories and navigation.

MAKE ITCup constellation projector
03🌙
Moon Phases

Moon in Motion

See how sunlight makes the Moon appear to change—without the Moon changing shape.

MAKE ITPaper-plate Moon phase slider
04☀️
Scale & Distance

A Solar System You Can Walk

Shrink enormous space distances into a model and discover why interplanetary travel is hard.

MAKE ITWalkable scale model
05🚀
Newton’s Laws

Ready, Set, Launch!

Experiment with force, motion, and action–reaction just like real rocket engineers.

MAKE ITBalloon rocket race
06⏳
Gravity

Gravity Keeps the Beat

Build a pendulum, make predictions, and test how its length changes the rhythm of each swing.

MAKE ITWorking pendulum lab
